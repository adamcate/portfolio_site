2021-2022 NASA COSGC Robotics Challenge

As part of the NASA robotics challenge, our team was given a semester to design, construct, and program a robot which could autonomously navigate the Great Sand Dunes.

We defined our initial requirements as follows:

  • Mass of no more than 4 kg
  • Resilient to rough & uneven terrain
  • Autonomous navigation loop
  • No external navigation references (i.e. GPS)

Through trade studies, we determined that a rocker bogie suspension system, similar to the ones used on real life Mars rovers, would be a good fit. A differential bar is used to link the rotations of two rocker arms, ensuring that the two sides can actuate in response to uneven obstacles. The rocker arms house the front wheels, and the attached bogie (mounted on a hinged joint) allows for the remaining 2 wheels on each side to stay in contact with the ground.
